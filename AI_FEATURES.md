## Legal Research Assistant

### Overview

The Legal Research Assistant is an AI-powered tool that provides instant answers to U.S. legal questions with verified citations from authoritative sources. It delivers structured legal analysis in clear language with proper Bluebook-style citations.

### Key Features

#### Verified Legal Citations
- **Supreme Court Cases**: Full citations with reporter information
- **Federal Statutes**: U.S.C. citations with sections and subsections
- **Regulations**: C.F.R. citations
- **State Law**: Major state statutes and case law
- **Constitutional Provisions**: Amendments and articles
- **Pinpoint Citations**: Specific page references included

#### Advanced Filtering
- **Jurisdiction Filter**: Federal or State law
- **Date Range**: Filter cases by date
- **Court Level**: Supreme Court, Circuit, District, State Courts
- **Judge Filter**: Search by specific judge name
- **Clear Filters**: Reset all filters with one click

#### Response Structure

Every research response includes four sections:

**1. SUMMARY**
- Plain English explanation of the legal issue
- 2-4 sentence overview
- Accessible to non-lawyers while legally accurate

**2. KEY AUTHORITIES**
- List of primary legal sources
- Properly formatted citations
- Supreme Court cases, statutes, regulations
- Constitutional provisions when relevant

**3. ANALYSIS**
- Detailed explanation of how law applies
- Historical context and legal principles
- Court interpretations and holdings
- Practical implications
- Conflicts between jurisdictions

**4. CITATIONS**
- Complete Bluebook-style citations
- Pinpoint references
- Full case names and reporter information
- Year and court identification

### How to Use

#### Basic Workflow

1. **Navigate**: Click "Legal Research" in AI Features sidebar
2. **Enter Question**: Type your legal question in the text area
3. **Apply Filters** (Optional):
   - Select jurisdiction (Federal/State)
   - Choose date range
   - Filter by court level
   - Enter judge name if relevant
4. **Submit**: Click "Research Question" button
5. **Review Results**: Read structured response
6. **Export**: Copy or print the response

#### Sample Questions

**Employment Law**:
- "What is the burden of proof in employment discrimination cases?"
- "What are the elements of a Title VII claim?"
- "When does the statute of limitations begin for wrongful termination?"

**Constitutional Law**:
- "What is the standard for qualified immunity?"
- "What are the requirements for a valid Fourth Amendment search?"
- "What level of scrutiny applies to First Amendment cases?"

**Contract Law**:
- "What are the elements of promissory estoppel?"
- "When is a contract unconscionable?"
- "What is the statute of frauds requirement?"

**Civil Procedure**:
- "What is the standard for summary judgment?"
- "What are the elements of personal jurisdiction?"
- "When does Rule 11 apply to sanctions?"

### Tips for Best Results

#### Writing Effective Questions

✅ **Do**:
- Be specific about the legal issue
- Include jurisdiction when relevant
- Reference specific statutes if known
- Ask about elements, defenses, or procedures
- Break complex questions into parts

❌ **Don't**:
- Ask for personal legal advice
- Submit vague or overly broad questions
- Rely solely on AI for case strategy
- Ask questions outside U.S. law
- Request predictions about specific cases

#### Using Filters

**Jurisdiction Filter**:
- Federal: Focus on U.S. Supreme Court and federal law
- State: Include state court decisions and statutes
- Use when you need specific jurisdiction analysis

**Date Range Filter**:
- Recent cases: Set date range to last 5-10 years
- Historical analysis: Expand date range
- Trending issues: Focus on recent years

**Court Level Filter**:
- Supreme Court: Binding precedent nationwide
- Circuit Courts: Binding in specific circuits
- District Courts: Persuasive authority
- State Courts: Binding in that state

**Judge Filter**:
- Research specific judge's decisions
- Find patterns in judicial reasoning
- Identify judge-authored opinions

#### Conclusion
